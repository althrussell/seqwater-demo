## 1. Purpose

To outline the synthetic principles for drafting communications to retailer customers
and other stakeholders during synthetic operational events. The protocol exists to
ensure communications are consistent, fact-based, and conservative.

## 2. Synthetic principles

1. Lead with verified facts. Avoid speculation.
2. State the source of synthetic data used in the communication.
3. State assumptions and known unknowns.
4. State the validation status of any AI-assisted draft.
5. Use plain language. Avoid alarmist tone.
6. Direct recipients to the appropriate human contact point.

## 3. Synthetic message blocks

A retailer customer communication should typically include:

- A short situation summary.
- The current synthetic operational posture.
- What this means for retailer operations.
- The recommended retailer-side preparedness or actions.
- How and when the next update will be issued.
- The named human contact point for questions.

## 4. AI assistant guidance

The AI assistant may produce a draft communication based on synthetic operational data.
The draft must:

- Be clearly labelled as a synthetic draft.
- Include the synthetic data sources that contributed.
- Include a named human reviewer placeholder.
- Avoid implying any operational decision has been authorised.

## 5. Human-in-the-loop validation

No retailer customer communication may be issued from this demo. Real communications
require approval through the appropriate Seqwater governance pathway.
