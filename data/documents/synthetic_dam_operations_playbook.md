# Dam Operations Playbook

**Status:** DEMO ARTEFACT — for executive demonstration only.
**Owner:** Bulk Water Operations
**Last reviewed:** 2026-05-15
**Classification:** Internal demo

> This playbook is an example created for a Databricks demo of the Seqwater
> AI Command Centre. It does not reflect real Seqwater operating procedures, thresholds,
> or escalation paths. Operational decisions must always be made by qualified Seqwater
> personnel using approved procedures, telemetry, and bureau forecasts.

---

## 1. Purpose

This playbook describes the operating posture for major bulk water dams during
the watch and respond phases of a developing rainfall and inflow event. It supports the
demonstration of how a governed AI assistant can summarise context, retrieve procedure
text, and propose conservative recommendations that are explicitly subject to
human-in-the-loop validation.

## 2. Operating posture levels

The posture model uses four bands. They are illustrative only.

| Band       | Trigger (illustrative)                          | Indicative response                                  |
|------------|-----------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------|
| Routine    | Storage well below operating threshold; no severe forecast| Continue scheduled inspections and routine releases.           |
| Watch      | Forecast rainfall raises catchment saturation indicators  | Confirm telemetry availability, brief duty hydrologist.        |
| Respond    | Storage approaching attention threshold         | Convene operations review, validate inflow projections.        |
| Coordinate | Multi-asset risk converging                     | Escalate to executive flood operations cell.                   |

## 3. Watch checklist

1. Confirm rainfall forecast inputs from at least two sources.
2. Verify dam telemetry health for storage, inflow, and outflow sensors.
3. Check upstream catchment monitoring sites are reporting.
4. Note any open critical work orders against impacted assets.
5. Brief the duty hydrologist and water quality lead.

## 4. Respond checklist

1. Validate latest inflow with manual gauge reading where possible.
2. Confirm dam release configuration is consistent with current operating manual.
3. Notify retailer customers per stakeholder communications protocol.
4. Pre-stage spares for critical pump stations on dependent supply chains.
5. Document the decision context, owners, and validation steps.

## 5. Communication

All external communications during a Watch or Respond posture must follow the
Retailer Customer Communications Protocol. Internal communications must be
factually grounded, refer to telemetry sources, and avoid unverified projections.

## 6. Human-in-the-loop validation

Any AI-assisted summary of dam operating posture must be reviewed by a qualified
hydrologist and the on-call operations lead before it is used to support an operational
decision. AI assistants must not autonomously approve releases, transfers, or
restrictions.
